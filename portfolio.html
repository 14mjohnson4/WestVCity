Utah Cultural Celebration Center

Constitution Boulevard

In 2003, the Utah Cultural Celebration Center (UCCC) was created to provide a place where people from all walks of life can come together and share ideas; inspire, nurture, and learn from one another; enjoy arts and cultural experiences; concerts and performing arts. It is also a place for businesses, individuals and other groups to gather for conferences, seminars, ceremonies, and other special events. Last year, public arts and cultural programs at the Center served nearly 56,000 patrons.

Unification

Parkway Park

Unification: the process of being united or made into a whole. This new mural is a collaboration with fellow artists and the community of mixed backgrounds and cultures. Each color and iconography represents 16 nation flags, spanning each continent into one single thread as well as the PRIDE colors, 210' X 6'. The monarch butterflies signify the migration of people and cultures to the United States and particularly to this neighborhood. Together we are stronger as we celebrate the diversity in our own communities and as individuals.

Artist: Josh Scheuerman, Bill Louis, The Roots Art Kollective, Mr. Vandal, and Dave Doman

Category: Mural

Thrown Bulldogger

2700 South

Julia Harmons work is an investigation into the past. Julia is interested in what has been discarded over time, such as personal memories, societal norms, and fashions of the times. Julias source material is often lifted from vintage magazines and books that portray cultural ideals, concepts which I disassociate from their original contexts. Julia is particularly interested in studying the conventional roles of women as illustrated in these sources, using her artwork to challenge and subvert these notions.

Artist: Julia Harmon

Category: Utility Box Wraps
